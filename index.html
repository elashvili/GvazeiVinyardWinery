Our Heritage

Born from
the ancient vine

Gvazi is a family winery whose story begins in Samegrelo, in the village of Nagvazao in the Martvili municipality — a place where vines and wine have been a natural part of life for centuries. The root of both our family name and village — "vaz," meaning vine (Gvazava, Nagvazao) — reflects the deep bond between our family and the tradition of winemaking.

In 1993, following the wars in Abkhazia and South Ossetia, our grandfather Abel Gvazava renewed the family vineyards and continued the tradition passed down through generations. Since 2019, we have been actively cultivating endemic Megrelian grape varieties across around 4 hectares of limestone-rich mineral clay soils.

Our winery is in Okrokhana, Tbilisi, near Mtatsminda Park, on a south-eastern slope. The qvevri cellar spans three terraces, allowing wine to move by gravity from qvevri to qvevri with zero mechanical intervention — preserving natural balance and minimising stress.
